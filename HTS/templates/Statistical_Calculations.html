About Statistical Calculations

Mean (x̄)

The arithmetic average of all data points. It's calculated by summing all values and dividing by the number of values.

Formula: x̄ = (Σxᵢ) / n

Where Σxᵢ is the sum of all values and n is the number of values.

Variance (s²)

Measures how far each number in the set is from the mean. It's the average of the squared differences from the Mean.

Formula: s² = Σ(xᵢ - x̄)² / n

For sample variance (when calculating from a sample of a larger population), we divide by (n-1) instead of n.

Standard Deviation (s)

A measure of the amount of variation or dispersion in a set of values. It's the square root of the variance.

Formula: s = √(s²)

A low standard deviation indicates that values tend to be close to the mean, while a high standard deviation indicates values are spread out.
